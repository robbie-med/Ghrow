How this app works

The app is static. It loads a curve catalog from data/catalog.json, fetches the selected CSV file, plots percentile columns from that file, and uses LMS values in the same rows to estimate Z-scores and percentiles for entered measurements.

Saved patient measurements are stored only in the browser. The data files are separate from the JavaScript so the raw source can be inspected, replaced, versioned, and cited.

Clinical caution: this is not a diagnostic device. Use corrected age, curve choice, gestational-age-specific curves, and local policy carefully.

Special populations — when to use which reference

Alongside WHO 2006 and CDC 2000, the plotter now includes condition- and population-specific references. Pick a measure (e.g. Weight for age) and then the matching source. The notes below describe when each is indicated — general guidance only; always consult the primary publication and local policy.

Fenton 2003

integrated

When to use: preterm infants from ~22 to 50 weeks postmenstrual age (gestational + postnatal) — in the NICU and after discharge — for weight, length, and head circumference. Transition to WHO 2006 around term-corrected age.

Best for: very-low-birth-weight and preterm infants, where term age-based curves do not apply. Enter a gestational age to plot points by postmenstrual age.

Source: Fenton TR. BMC Pediatrics 2003;3:13 — LMS tables via peditools.

INTERGROWTH-21st (preterm)

integrated

When to use: the Postnatal Growth of Preterm Infants standards (27–64 weeks postmenstrual age), as a prescriptive international alternative to Fenton, for weight, length, and head circumference.

Best for: preterm postnatal growth in settings that prefer the INTERGROWTH-21st international standard. (Newborn Size-for-GA tables are not yet wired in.)

Source: Villar J, et al. Lancet Glob Health 2015 — centile tables via gigs.

Down syndrome (Zemel 2015)

integrated

When to use: children with Trisomy 21, birth–20 years (weight, length/height, head circumference, BMI). The AAP recommends plotting on standard WHO/CDC charts and Down-syndrome-specific charts.

Best for: condition-specific surveillance alongside the standard charts, accounting for shorter stature and different weight/BMI trajectories.

Source: Zemel BS, et al. Pediatrics 2015;136:e1204 — LMS tables via peditools.

China NHC 2022

integrated

When to use: children in China or of Chinese descent, birth–7 years (weight, height, head circumference, BMI, weight-for-height/length), where a national reference reflects the population better than WHO/CDC.

Best for: population-specific monitoring where the national standard is standard of care.

Source: National Health Commission of China WS/T 423-2022 — parameter tables via groowooth.

Korea KNGC2017

not yet integrated

When to use: children in Korea or of Korean descent, 0–18 years, using the 2017 Korean National Growth Charts (same LMS method as the other references).

Best for: population-specific monitoring in Korea.

Source: KDCA — official LMS parameter tables at the KDCA growth-chart portal. Pending a machine-readable export.

Data sources & toolkits

The reference tables in this app are derived from these open publications and toolkits. The generator script scripts/build-special-curves.py documents exactly how each CSV is produced.

  • SASPAC/gigs — INTERGROWTH-21st (and WHO) standards; source of the preterm postnatal centile tables.
  • jhchou/peditools — source of the Fenton 2003 and Zemel 2015 Down-syndrome LMS tables.
  • xiaot945/groowooth — China NHC WS/T 423-2022 parameter tables.
  • childsds (Leipzig source) — curated multi-reference LMS collection, consulted for provenance.
  • KDCA growth-chart portal — official Korean KNGC2017 tables (integration pending).
